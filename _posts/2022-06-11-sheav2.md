## Sheaf Cohomology
If $X$ is a topological space, the group of sections defines functors $\Gamma \colon \mathbf{pSh} (X) \to \mathbf{Ab}$ and $\Gamma \colon \mathbf{Sh} (X) \to \mathbf{Ab}$. In either case, however, the functor $\Gamma$ is defined on objects $X$ by \\[ \Gamma \colon X \mapsto \Gamma (X, \mathscr{F}) = \mathscr{F} (U) \\] Furthermore, if we let $\varphi \colon \mathscr{F} \to \mathscr{G}$ be a (pre)sheaf of maps—where for each open $U$ in $X$, we have $\varphi(U) \colon \mathscr{F}(U) \to \mathscr{G}(U)$—the global section functor on these maps is given by $\Gamma \colon \varphi (s) \mapsto \varphi_X (s)$, where $s \in \Gamma(X, \mathscr{F})$.
